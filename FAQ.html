What is a microinternship?

A microinternship is a short-term, professional assignment that is similar to those given to new hires or interns. These projects enable students to demonstrate skills, explore career paths, and build their networks as they seek the right full-time role.

Who can apply for a Plum microinternship?

Microinternships through Plum are designed for CT community college students looking to gain practical experience in their field of study or explore new career paths. While we are focused on STEM majors and projects, all majors are welcome to apply

How do businesses benefit from offering a microinternship?

Businesses gain access to a pool of motivated, skilled students ready to tackle short-term projects. This not only helps with immediate business needs but also strengthens community ties and supports the local economy. It's a cost-effective way to innovate and complete projects while mentoring the next generation of professionals.

How long does a microinternship last?

Microinternships on our platform typically last between a few days to a few weeks, depending on the project's scope and the business's needs. This flexibility allows students to work around their academic schedules and businesses to address their project needs efficiently

Are Plum microinternships paid?

Yes, all microinternships facilitated through Plum are paid opportunities. We believe in compensating students for their contributions and ensuring fair compensation practices for their work and time. Businesses determine how much they would like to pay a student for a project, we recommend a lump sum equal to $18 - 23 an hour. Projects are paid out in full after project completion.

How does Plum ensure a good match between students and businesses?

Plum uses a comprehensive matching process that considers the skills, interests, and academic background of students, as well as the specific needs and project requirements of businesses. This tailored approach ensures that both parties benefit maximally from the partnership.

What kind of support does Plum offer to students and businesses during the microinternship?

Plum provides continuous support throughout the microinternship process, including resources for best practices, communication tools, and resume writing strategies. We are committed to ensuring a positive and productive experience for both students and businesses. Project funds are held in escrow until prpject completion, it you are unsatisfied with your experience we will issue a full refund.

Can microinternships lead to full time employment?

While microinternships are primarily learning and development opportunities, they can sometimes lead to full-time job offers if there's a mutual fit and business need. However, there's no obligation for businesses to offer employment or for students to accept offers post-microinternship.

Does Plum handle payments for microinterns if they transition to full time roles?

Plum facilitates the initial connection between students and businesses for microinternships, including handling payments for the microinternship itself through the third-party payroll service Square. However, should a business decide to hire a microintern for a full-time role, the responsibility for compensation transitions to the business. Plum does not manage payments for positions beyond the scope of the microinternship project.

Is there a fee for hiring a microintern into a full time position?

No, Plum does not charge businesses a fee for hiring a microintern into a full- time role. Our mission is to foster meaningful partnerships that benefit both community college students and local businesses. We believe in supporting the transition from microinternships to employment without additional financial barriers.

How does Plum ensure the safety of its interns?

The safety of our interns is paramount. Plum conducts thorough vetting of all participating businesses to ensure they provide a safe and supportive environment for our students. We require businesses to agree to our code of conduct, which includes adherence to all local laws and regulations regarding workplace safety and intern treatment. Additionally, we offer support and resources to both students and businesses to address any concerns or issues that may arise during the course of a microinternship.
